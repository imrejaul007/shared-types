STRATEGIC PRODUCT DOCUMENT

REZ Consumer App — Full UI/UX Plan

Based on: Daily Habit Loop Research · Bangalore Micro-Moment Map · Magicpin / Dineout Failure Analysis

🎯
Core thesis from your research: Daily usage doesn't come from features. It comes from owning a micro-moment — the 10-second decision window where a user thinks "where should I eat / go / spend right now?" ReZ must become the default app opened in that moment. This UI/UX plan is built entirely around this principle.
1 Research Synthesis
What the document tells us — distilled into design requirements
🔥 5 Triggers That Create Daily Opens
  1. Daily spending decisions — "where to eat/go today"
  2. Savings tracking psychology — "₹480 saved this week, ₹120 more to VIP"
  3. Time-limited urgency — "lunch deals live now, slots filling"
  4. Trial discovery habit — "new cafe drop today" (Smytten model, hyperlocal)
  5. Gamification progress — streak visible, next reward close
🚨 Why Magicpin / Dineout / Nearbuy Failed
  • Solved discovery, not decision
  • Wide supply, shallow micro-cluster depth
  • Cashback invisible — not cumulative, not emotional
  • No time-contextual urgency (same feed all day)
  • Gamification without social context (solo = boring)
  • Generic notifications → muted → habit broken
  • Brand confusion: deals + payment + discovery + gaming all at once

7 New Components Required
Components that don't exist yet but are critical for the daily habit loop
NEW P0 Critical
MicroMomentDecisionCard
Purpose: The single most important component. Answers "where do I go right now?" at the top of the screen — before savings, before gamification.

Shows: Live count of options within 500m for current time slot (e.g. "7 lunch spots near campus"), sorted by savings, with one-tap access.

Data needed: nearbyApi.getDecisionFeed(lat, lon, timeSlot, persona)

Design: White card, mustard left border, bold count at top, single CTA. Changes copy + color by time slot (morning = green, lunch = amber, evening = purple, night = dark).
NEW P0 Critical
StreakToDealConnector
Purpose: Connects the streak counter to a specific action today. Right now, the streak card is just a counter — this component tells the user what the streak unlocks TODAY, not just "progress".

Shows: "You're on a [N]-day streak. Here's your streak bonus at [Merchant Name]" with a countdown.

Only shows when: Streak ≥ 3 days AND user hasn't redeemed today's streak deal.

Design: Mustard border, warm amber background, single deal card with CTA.
NEW P0 Critical
PersonaDetectionOnboarding
Purpose: Detects student vs employee on Day 1 to enable personalized copy throughout the app. Currently every user sees 'general' persona.

Implementation: One-time 3-option card on first home screen visit: Student / Employee / General. Stores result in user profile (backend) + Zustand store (frontend).

Used by: TimeAwareContextPill, MicroMomentDecisionCard, notification scheduler.

Design: Light gray background card with 3 tappable tiles. Dismisses after selection. Never shows again.
NEW P1 High
LiveNearbyCount (upgrade to TimeAwareContextPill)
Purpose: Replace hardcoded "14 offers within 1 km" copy with live data from the nearby offers API.

Implementation: Add a nearbyCount?: number prop. Caller (index.tsx) fetches real count from nearbyApi.getCount() and passes it. Falls back gracefully to generic copy if API fails.

Why critical: The urgency ("14 offers near you") only creates a habit trigger if it's real. Fake numbers kill trust within 2 weeks.
NEW P1 High
CoinExpiryUrgencyBanner
Purpose: Creates loss aversion — the most powerful habit trigger. "You have 120 RC expiring in 12 hours" drives opens better than any reward promise.

Shows only when: User has coins expiring in <48 hours.

Data: From existing wallet/coin API — just needs surface in a high-visibility component.

Design: Red-tinted card, expiry countdown, direct "Use before expiry →" CTA.
NEW P2 Medium
SocialProofNearby
Purpose: "3 people from your area checked in here today" — the social trigger that Magicpin attempted but failed to make contextual.

Student version: Campus leaderboard teaser — "You're #3 saver on campus this week."

Employee version: Office cluster activity — "5 colleagues used REZ Pay at lunch today."

Data needed: Backend aggregation by location cluster (anonymized).

9 Notification Strategy

11 What Will Kill Daily Habit (Anti-Patterns to Avoid)
From your research on Magicpin / Dineout / Nearbuy failures — translated to concrete do-nots
❌
Same feed every day. If the home screen looks identical on Monday and Thursday, users stop checking. Every open must feel like something might be new or time-sensitive.
❌
Empty nearby supply. If the Decision Card shows "0 deals near you" even once, the user loses trust permanently. Only show this card when live data confirms supply.
❌
Notification spam. 3 generic notifications in a row = permanent mute. After that, no trigger can bring the user back. Enforce 2/day hard limit + persona filtering in backend.
❌
Delayed cashback / fake urgency. If user clicks "12 slots left" and finds 50 slots — or cashback takes 7 days — trust is broken. Urgency must be real. Cashback must feel instant.
✅
Win one micro-moment first. For Bangalore launch: student lunch (12PM) and employee lunch (12PM) are the highest-frequency daily decisions. Win these completely before expanding.
✅
Make savings feel cumulative and emotional. "You've saved ₹1,240 this month" hits different than "You saved ₹80 today." The monthly journey creates pride and investment in the app.
✅
Streak → specific deal connector. The streak counter only drives daily opens when the user knows what the streak earns TODAY, not just a vague "progress" number.
✅
Loss aversion > gain promise. "120 coins expiring in 12 hours" drives more opens than "Earn 50 extra coins today." Build the expiry urgency banner as P1.
🎯 One-Page Summary
REZ can become a daily app, but only if it owns the "where should I spend right now?" micro-moment. The current home page has the right bones (TimeAwareContextPill, DailyCheckInStrip, VisitStreakCard, savings hero) but three critical intelligence layers are off: (1) persona detection is hardcoded to 'general' — no student/employee personalization fires in production; (2) nearby counts and urgency signals are static copy — users learn to ignore them within days; (3) the streak counter has no "here's your reward today" connector, making it a number that feels meaningless. The P0 work is to turn on the intelligence that is already architecturally present. The P1 work is to add the StreakToDeal connector and the CoinExpiryUrgency trigger — these two components alone will move the daily retention needle more than any new feature.
